The Vision

Designed for presence.
Not performance.

This is not a vacation rental. It is a philosophy made physical — the belief that the best life is a quiet one, lived close to something larger than yourself. Every room serves the body, the mind, or the soul. Nothing else.

01
No Screens
No televisions. No notifications. Chalkboards, books, games, and conversation replace every screen.
02
All Natural
Every surface is stone, raw wood, fired clay, or woven linen. Nothing synthetic. The home breathes with you.
03
Human Scale
Built for a small circle. Intimate enough to feel personal, spacious enough to breathe. A lodge that holds you.
04
Mountain First
Ski-in ski-out. Every room faces the slope or the peaks. The mountain is not the backdrop — it is the reason.
The Rooms

Every room has a purpose.
Every purpose serves the human.

Room 01
The Pool & Sauna
A small dark stone heated pool beside a cedar-lined sauna. One skylight. Steam rising. No lanes, no lengths — just water to float in, think in, and recover in after a full day on the mountain.
Dark stone basin — cedar sauna — river stone floor — no screens
Room 02
The Yoga & Meditation Room
A bare room on the second floor. Clay plaster walls. Two linen mats on warm stone. A single window facing the entire mountain range. The kind of room that slows your heart rate the moment you enter.
Clay walls — heated stone floor — mountain window — singing bowl
Room 03
The Après Bar & Lounge
A deep linen sectional sofa beside a stone open bar with copper taps. Large plants in clay pots. A large abstract oil painting above the sofa. Skis off. Always open to friends.
Stone bar — copper taps — linen sofa — clay planters — abstract art
Room 04
The Art Room
A raw wood worktable covered in paint marks and charcoal dust. Canvases leaning on every dark stone wall. An unfinished painting on an easel facing the north window. The room itself is the art piece.
Raw worktable — north-facing light — clay plaster walls — no technology
Room 05
The Main Bedroom
A low dark oak platform bed. Deep linen bedding and layered wool throws. A candle and a paperback on the bedside table. Floor-to-ceiling mountain window. Designed only for deep rest and waking slowly to a view.
Dark oak bed — linen — mountain window — no alarm clock — no screens
Room 06
The Bathroom
A deep freestanding stone soaking tub. River stone floor. Copper fixtures. Eucalyptus hanging from the shower rod. A small watercolor beside the tub — soft blues and greys bleeding into each other.
Stone tub — copper fixtures — river stone — eucalyptus — handmade ceramics
